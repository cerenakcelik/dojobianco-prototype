Grounding — Meeting the Earth Within

A gentle Dojo Bianco introduction to breath, weight, and the support beneath you.

At Dojo Bianco, grounding isn’t a technique — it’s a meeting. Imagine the cool stone beneath your feet, olive branches moving in a slow breeze, and your breath arriving without effort. We’re simply remembering that the body knows how to be here.

How to begin

Stand or sit comfortably. Let your shoulders drop 2 millimeters. Feel the gentle weight of your body moving downwards. Inhale softly through the nose. Exhale a little longer than you inhale. Three minutes is enough.

Self-assessment (before practice)

1) Body connection right now

Choose the phrase that feels closest.

  • Very connected — Beautiful. Stay close to sensation; widen your field rather than “doing more.”
  • Somewhat connected — Good. Today is about consistency over intensity.
  • Disconnected — Welcome. Nothing to fix. Let gravity do the first step.
2) Breath pattern

What describes your breath?

  • Slow & steady — Keep the exhale softer and slightly longer; no forcing.
  • Shallow / chest — Invite breath to the back ribs; imagine space behind the heart.
  • Held / irregular — Start with three sighs out of the mouth; then return to the nose.
3) Anchor awareness

Where do you feel most anchored?

  • Feet / legs — Excellent. Let the pelvis get 1% heavier.
  • Pelvis / belly — Good. Let the jaw soften; tongue rests on the floor of the mouth.
  • Head / thoughts — Perfect place to start. Bring attention down to the soles; feel temperature + pressure.
Practice (3 minutes)
  1. Feel the contact: soles ↔ stone / earth.
  2. Inhale softly. Exhale slightly longer. Let shoulders and belly receive gravity.
  3. Notice one detail around you (light on wall, a distant sound). Let it slow you.
Reflection
  • What felt more available — breath, weight, or space?
  • Where did your body say “yes” to grounding?
  • One sentence you want to remember for later.
Overall insight

Grounding is not about being heavy — it’s about being related. Today’s meeting with the earth is enough. Tomorrow, we meet again.
