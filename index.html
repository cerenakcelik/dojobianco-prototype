Grounding — Meeting the Earth Within

At Dojo Bianco, grounding isn’t a technique — it’s a meeting. Imagine the cool stone beneath your feet, olive branches moving in a slow breeze, and your breath arriving without effort. We’re simply remembering that the body knows how to be here.

How to begin

Stand or sit comfortably. Let your shoulders drop 2 millimeters. Feel the gentle weight of your body moving downwards. Inhale softly through the nose. Exhale a little longer than you inhale. Three minutes is enough.

Self-assessment (before practice)

1) Body connection right now

Choose the phrase that feels closest.

  • Very connected — Beautiful. Stay close to sensation; widen your field rather than “doing more.”
  • Somewhat connected — Good. Today is about consistency over intensity.
  • Disconnected — Welcome. Nothing to fix. Let gravity do the first step.
2) Breath pattern

What describes your breath?

  • Slow & steady — Keep the exhale softer and slightly longer; no forcing.
  • Shallow / in the chest — Invite breath to the back ribs; imagine space behind the heart.
  • Held / irregular — Begin with three sighs out of the mouth; then return to the nose.
3) Anchor awareness

Where do you feel most anchored?

  • Feet / legs — Excellent. Let the pelvis get 1% heavier.
  • Pelvis / belly — Good. Let the jaw soften; tongue rests on the floor of the mouth.
  • Head / thoughts — Perfect place to start. Bring attention down to the soles; feel temperature + pressure.
Practice (3 minutes)
  1. Feel the contact: soles ↔ stone / earth.
  2. Inhale softly. Exhale slightly longer. Let shoulders and belly receive gravity.
  3. Notice one detail around you (light on wall, a distant sound). Let it slow you.
Reflection
  • What felt more available — breath, weight, or space?
  • Where did your body say “yes” to grounding?
  • One sentence you want to remember for later.
Overall insight

Grounding is not about being heavy — it’s about being related. Today’s meeting with the earth is enough. Tomorrow, we meet again.

Embodied Presence — Returning to the Living Body

Presence is not achieved by effort; it emerges when the mind relaxes into the body’s truth. In Embodied Presence we practice coming home to sensation, emotion, and breath — as they are — without forcing a change. This is the ground of honest contact with ourselves and with others.

Self-assessment
  • Mind–body connection: Mostly in thoughts / Moving between / Present with sensation.
  • Emotional awareness: Clear / Muted / Overwhelmed.
  • Contact with environment: Narrow / Balanced / Wide (add one anchor).
Practice (5 minutes)
  1. Sense map: name three sensations (pressure, temperature, movement).
  2. Emotion check: which feeling is closest — joy, sadness, fear, anger, calm, or not sure?
  3. Breath bridge: on the exhale, let the feeling be held by the breath rather than the mind.
  4. Contact: include one external sound and the support beneath you.
“Presence is not achieved. It is remembered — each time we meet ourselves in honesty.” — Dojo Bianco Teaching

Chakras & Energy — A Calm Introduction

The human energy field is the bridge between consciousness and matter. In this Brennan-inspired overview, chakras are understood as transformers of energy between body and subtle fields — practical, experiential, and grounded.

Seven Centers (brief)
  • Root — safety & belonging · Practice: feel soles and base of spine.
  • Sacral — flow & pleasure · Practice: belly breath, gentle pelvis mobility.
  • Solar Plexus — will & clarity · Practice: warmth around navel and ribs.
  • Heart — love & balance · Practice: breathe behind the sternum.
  • Throat — truth & expression · Practice: hum softly; relax jaw.
  • Brow — insight & perspective · Practice: space behind eyes.
  • Crown — connection & meaning · Practice: awareness up, return to heart.
Self-assessment
  • Which center feels most alive today?
  • Where do you notice tightness or numbness?
  • Where is breath most available?
Practice — Vertical Alignment (7 min)

Sense a central column of light (hara). Breathe crown → root; exhale through the feet. Let each center harmonize. End by resting awareness at the heart.

“Energy flows where honesty meets awareness.” — Dojo Bianco Teaching

From the Leaving Pattern to Creative Pleasure

When the first contact with life feels unsafe, the spirit learns to hover near the doorway.
Wound

Shattered attention of the incoming spirit; hostility in-utero to 6 months. Fear of embodiment and annihilation — “my life force threatens my life.”

Main Issues

Insufficient grounding, fragile attachment, chronic fear of existence, split between thought and sensation.

Defenses

Denial, projection, escape, withdrawal, mental defense, limited contact, avoidance of feeling, freeze, dissociation.

Patterns

Hypervigilance; leaves at first sign of trouble; seeks safety in solitude or nature; over-sensitive to other’s energy; self-harm; rage; anxiety.

Blocks to Joy

Inner critic/self-hatred; weak energetic boundaries; energy shifting upward to head and read as fear; overwhelm and confusion that block joy.

Tasks & Real Needs

Safety and belonging in a chosen tribe; come fully into the body; individuate; integrate life experience.

Higher Gifts

Expanded psychic perception; visionary thinking; powerful creativity when grounded — inventors, artists, musicians.

Healing Responses

Light, agenda-free touch; first connect through rational mind; add playfulness, movement, dance, music, grounding, mind-body therapy and anger work.

Path through the 4 D’s to Joy
  1. Hara — align intention to remember safety & belonging.
  2. Core — embody spiritual aspects in the physical world.
  3. Auric — gently dissolve the superego/inner critic; install supportive adult ego.
  4. Expansion — return to the creative life pulse (Joy).
“I am safe to create in this body.” — Dojo Bianco Teaching
