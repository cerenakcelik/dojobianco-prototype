Grounding — Meeting the Earth Within›

At Dojo Bianco, grounding isn’t a technique — it’s a meeting. Imagine the cool stone beneath your feet, olive branches moving in a slow breeze, and your breath arriving without effort. We’re simply remembering that the body knows how to be here.

How to begin

Stand or sit comfortably. Let your shoulders drop 2 millimeters. Feel the gentle weight of your body moving downwards. Inhale softly through the nose. Exhale a little longer than you inhale. Three minutes is enough.

Self-assessment
  • Very connected — widen awareness without doing more.
  • Somewhat connected — consistency over intensity.
  • Disconnected — welcome; let gravity lead.
Practice (3 min)
  1. Feel contact: soles ↔ stone / earth.
  2. Exhale slightly longer; let shoulders and belly receive gravity.
  3. Notice one detail (light, sound). Let it slow you.
Reflection
  • What felt more available — breath, weight, or space?
  • Where did your body say “yes” to grounding?
  • One sentence to remember.
Embodied Presence — Returning to the Living Body›

Presence is not achieved by effort; it emerges when the mind relaxes into the body’s truth. We practice coming home to sensation, emotion, and breath — as they are — without forcing change. This is the ground of honest contact.

Self-assessment
  • Mind–body: Thoughts / Moving between / With sensation
  • Emotion: Clear / Muted / Overwhelmed
  • Contact: Narrow / Balanced / Wide (add one anchor)
Practice (5 min)
  1. Sense map: 3 sensations (pressure, temperature, movement).
  2. Emotion check: joy, sadness, fear, anger, calm, not sure.
  3. Breath bridge: let feeling be held by breath, not mind.
  4. Include one external sound and the support beneath you.
“Presence is not achieved. It is remembered — each time we meet ourselves in honesty.” — Dojo Bianco Teaching
Chakras & Energy — A Calm Introduction›

The human energy field is the bridge between consciousness and matter. In this Brennan-inspired overview, chakras are understood as transformers between body and subtle fields — practical, experiential, and grounded.

Seven Centers (brief)
  • Root — safety & belonging · feel soles and base of spine.
  • Sacral — flow & pleasure · belly breath, gentle pelvis.
  • Solar Plexus — will & clarity · warmth around navel/ribs.
  • Heart — love & balance · breathe behind the sternum.
  • Throat — truth & expression · hum softly; relax jaw.
  • Brow — insight & perspective · space behind eyes.
  • Crown — connection & meaning · awareness up, return to heart.
Practice — Vertical Alignment (7 min)

Sense a central column of light (hara). Breathe crown → root; exhale through the feet. Let each center harmonize. Rest awareness at the heart.

“Energy flows where honesty meets awareness.” — Dojo Bianco Teaching

From the Leaving Pattern to Creative Pleasure
When the first contact with life feels unsafe, the spirit learns to hover near the doorway.
Wound

Shattered attention of the incoming spirit; hostility in-utero to 6 months. Fear of embodiment and annihilation — “my life force threatens my life.”

Main Issues

Insufficient grounding, fragile attachment, chronic fear of existence, split between thought and sensation.

Defenses

Denial, projection, escape, withdrawal, mental defense, limited contact, avoidance of feeling, freeze, dissociation.

Patterns

Hypervigilance; leaves at first sign of trouble; seeks safety in solitude/nature; over-sensitive to others’ energy; self-harm; rage; anxiety.

Blocks to Joy

Inner critic/self-hatred; weak energetic boundaries; energy shifts upward to head (read as fear); overwhelm and confusion that block joy.

Tasks & Real Needs

Safety and belonging in a chosen tribe; come fully into the body; individuate; integrate life experience.

Higher Gifts

Expanded psychic perception; visionary thinking; powerful creativity when grounded — inventors, artists, musicians.

Healing Responses

Light, agenda-free touch; first connect through rational mind; add playfulness, movement, dance, music, grounding, mind-body therapy and anger work.

Path through the 4 D’s to Joy
  1. Hara — align intention to remember safety & belonging.
  2. Core — embody spiritual aspects in the physical world.
  3. Auric — gently dissolve the superego/inner critic; install supportive adult ego.
  4. Expansion — return to the creative life pulse (Joy).
